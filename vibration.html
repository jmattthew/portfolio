This tool allows product designers to create and test complex haptic patterns and to discover how the patterns actually feel in their hands. The tool allows precise control of vibration, much like a sound-wave synthesizer.

Android devices give responsive web developers access to the device's vibration motor and the ability provide users with haptic feedback. While haptic feedback has traditionally been reserved for OS functions such as notification-alerts and key-presses, product designers are free to incorporate haptic feedback into their websites. However, until now, it was very difficult to produce unique haptic patterns (e.g. a vibration that fades softly to full strength) because the browser cannot directly control the strength nor frequency of the device's vibration motor. The motor can merely be turned on at full strength for the specified duration.

I wanted to explore when and what type of haptic feedback might be appropriate within the browser setting, so a envisioned and coded this tool to experiment with haptic patterns. I realized that complex haptic patterns could be created by very rapidly cycling the vibration motor ON & OFF. By changing the duration of the ON & OFF phases of each cycle - much like a Morse code - the "attack" & "decay" of vibration could be controlled exactly like a very low frequency sound wave.
